JAPAN ANECDOTES

I have had a blog about trips to Japan for several years. At first, it was only a place to share my experiences travelling around the country, but it turned into a larger project: a platform where travellers contacted me to plan their trips to Japan.

Acting as a travel consultant, I spoke via Zoom with clients (mostly from the USA) to understand their preferences and tastes and answer questions. From there, I would make an initial design of their itinerary and we would discuss it and improve it until the client was satisfied. Only when the design was perfect, I would put together a personalized guide for each traveller and take care of accommodation and activity reservations.

Once in Japan, clients could contact me at any time if they needed help. For example, one time, when the Japan Meteorological Agency issued a typhoon warning for the following day at a client's holiday spot, I stayed up all night and changed her entire route to get her out of danger... as well as a plan C so she could choose her preferred option. I place a lot of importance on a client having the best possible experience when I am working with them, and of course not failing in the trust that has been given to me.

Since Covid I no longer accept new clients, so the site has remained a hobby project where I only work on the blog when I have the chance to visit Japan personally.


The current website is made with Wordpress, although it is being improved with an HTML/CSS version that will soon replace the current website. I have also designed a travel guide including maps, illustrations and photos for those who choose to prepare their own trip.
